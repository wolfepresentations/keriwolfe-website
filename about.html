About Me

Engineer. Researcher. Communicator.

I have a passion for effective communication and helping you discover how to best express your ideas. My background has mostly focused on technical content, but I've worked with non-technical presentations as well. I would love to hear from you!

My work is rooted in the Assertion-Evidence approach, developed by Michael Alley at Penn State, a research-backed method that dramatically improves how audiences comprehend and retain information from slides. You can learn more at the links below.

Background

Education

Originally from Pennsylvania, Keri graduated from Penn State University in 2013 with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering, a minor in German, and Honors in Communication Arts & Sciences. As a Penn State Engineering Ambassador, she delivered frequent presentations to diverse audiences ranging from 4 to 400 in size.

Research

During her undergraduate years, Keri worked closely with Michael Alley conducting research on the Assertion-Evidence method and presented at the 2011 and 2012 American Society for Engineering Education National Conferences. She completed her thesis and first-authored a paper in the field of Effective Engineering Communication.

Career

Keri is currently working as a technology consultant. Past roles include product management in the software industry, project management with an engineering consulting firm, and engineering roles in the energy industry. She has lived and worked in both the US and Germany and is currently based in Houston, TX.
